FAQs about scholarships China and more

1. What types of scholarships are available for international students in China? ▾

China offers full scholarships, partial scholarships, and exchange program scholarships. These may cover tuition, accommodation, living expenses, and sometimes airfare.

2. What are the requirements to apply for Chinese Scholarship? ▾

It depends on the scholarship you are applying but mainly is you must be an international student (not a Chinese citizen). You must meet all of the requirements to study in your chosen field in China. Your application should be recommended by your home country’s official education authority or foundation.

3. How many scholarships are available every year? ▾

China has about 51,000 scholarships every year. You can check the scholarships in our website and research for more information.

4. When is the application period for scholarships in China? ▾

Most Chinese scholarships open between January and April each year, but some universities and provincial programs may have different deadlines.

5. Do I need to know Chinese to apply for a scholarship? ▾

Not always. Many programs are offered in English. However, if you choose a Chinese-taught program, you may need to pass the HSK test (Chinese proficiency exam). However, it is also important to know some basic phrases because only few people use english outside campus. If you want to improve your chinese skills, we offer you some of the learning sources that you can go check it out in Courses page.

6. Are living expenses in China covered by scholarships? ▾

For full scholarships, a monthly stipend is usually provided for living costs. Partial scholarships may not cover living expenses, so you may need additional funds.

7. When is the application period for scholarships in China? ▾

Most Chinese scholarships open between January and April each year, but some universities and provincial programs may have different deadlines which you need to see on the scholarship program that you want to apply (you can go check some of them in our Scholarship page).

8. Can I use Instagram, Facebook, Tiktok, Youtube, etc. in China? ▾

Yes. But you need to have a VPN(if you are using China sim). the VPN we suggest is LetsVPN it may cost some money but it is the best one that always works.

9. What are the important apps to use in China? ▾

Alipay - an all in one app that could use in payments, taxi, post, etc. (better to create your account before going to China with your phone number and connect with your visa or mastercard. then after going, you can change to the chinese number and yor card will stay)
WeChat - For chatting, shopping, post photos, payment, etc.
美团 - for deliveries, groceries, food, and entertainment.
淘宝特价版 - the best marketplace that has everthing you need.
Weibo and 小红书 - optional but it is similar to twitter(X) or instagram for entertainment.

10. Do I need to prepare many stuffs to China? ▾

No, You don't need to bring much stuff(e.g. clothes, stationary, etc.) because they are usually cheaper in China. But the things you should be brining is ur favorite national food, mdicines you are familiar with, etc.

11. Do I need to change sim before going to China? ▾

Not necessary. You can use your own sim and buy a travel package (suggested for short terms that are less than a month). If the scholarship you chose is a lon-term one, we suggest to use a China sim.

Top 10 China Universities and its Brief Information

Tsinghua University

Tsinghua University established in 1911. It is located in northwest Beijing. It is the top 1 university in China. It covers many disciplines, for example, art, economics, education and more.

Peking University

Peking University is a member of C9 league(similar to the Ivy League Group in the US), generally one of the highest ranked in China. This university established in 1988. It is located in Beijing.

Fudan University

Fudan University is the third rank in China that is located in Shanghai. It comprises of for campuses in downtown Shanghai, which are Handan, Fenglin, Zhangjiang, and Jiangwan.

Zhejiang University

Zhejiang University founded in 1897 one of China's oldest and most selective universities. It is the fourth rank in China and covers 12 academic disciplines. It is located in Hangzhou, Zhejiang.

Shanghai Jiao Tong University

Shanghai Jiao Tong University, located in Shanghai. There are several subjects taught here, for example, mechanical engineering, physics astronomy, humanities, and others.

Univeristy of Science and Technology of China

University of Science and Technology of China located in Hefei, Anhui. It is the sixth ranked university.

Nanjing University

Nanjing University is located in Nanjing, Jiangsu. This university consider as the seventh ranked in China.

Wuhan University

Wuhan University is located in Harbin, Heilongjiang. It is consider as the eight ranked in China.

Beijing Normal University

Beijing Normal University, located in Beijing. This is the ninth ranked university in China.

Harbin Institute of Technology

Harbin Institute of Technology is located in Beijing. This is the tenth ranked university in China.
